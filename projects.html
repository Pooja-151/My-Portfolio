1. Class Sync App

Class Sync is an Android-based academic management app that allows students to organize subjects, notes, and syllabus in a structured, unit-wise format. The app features email authentication with login and signup functionality, and provides a clean dashboard for managing subjects, materials, and collaborative study groups. Users can upload notes and syllabus in PDF/PPT formats, create groups, and add members via email, where the group owner controls membership while all members can access and add study materials. All data is stored permanently using Room Database, and resources can also be shared within groups to support collaborative learning.

3. AI-Powered Marketplace Assistant for Local Artisans

The AI-Powered Marketplace Assistant for Local Artisans is a hackathon prototype developed by a collaborative team of five members. The idea behind this project is to bridge the gap between traditional artistry and modern digital platforms by equipping local artisans with AI-driven tools. Many artists struggle with creating promotional materials or managing the digital marketing side of their work, which often limits their reach and potential income. To address this, our platform offers multiple AI-powered features such as generating professional-quality posters, creating short promotional videos, and assisting with social media content including captions, bios, and relevant hashtags. Additionally, the system provides intelligent price suggestions to help artisans evaluate the fair and competitive value of their products in the market. By combining these tools into a single accessible web application, the project aims to reduce the marketing and branding challenges faced by artisans, giving them more time to focus on their creative work while still reaching a wider and more relevant audience.

4. Home Automationusing IOT and wireless technology

This IoT-based Smart Home project focuses on real-time automation, safety, and environmental monitoring using an ESP32 microcontroller and multiple sensors. The model integrates a rain sensor to detect rainfall and automatically close the rooftop shelter using a servo motor, protecting the interior from water exposure. In the living room section, a gas sensor monitors smoke levels; when smoke is detected, the ventilation fan turns on to improve air circulation. If the AQI level crosses a threshold of 2000, the buzzer activates to warn about unsafe air quality. Additionally, the fan can be controlled remotely via the Blynk application, enabling remote monitoring and smart automation through the Internet of Things. The project successfully demonstrates how IoT technology can enhance home safety, environmental awareness, and user convenience through intelligent control systems.
